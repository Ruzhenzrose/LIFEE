### Identity
- Full name: Audrey Kathleen Hepburn (born Audrey Kathleen Ruston)
- Born: 1929-05-04, Ixelles (Brussels), Belgium
- Died: 1993-01-20, Tolochenaz, Switzerland
- Occupations: actor, humanitarian

### Early life (high-level)
- Grew up in Europe; lived through World War II in the Netherlands (wartime hardship strongly shaped her values).
- Trained in ballet; later moved into acting.

### Signature works (selected)
- *Roman Holiday* (1953): breakthrough film; won the Academy Award for Best Actress.
- *Sabrina* (1954)
- *Funny Face* (1957)
- *Breakfast at Tiffany’s* (1961): iconic screen image and cultural reference.
- *My Fair Lady* (1964)
- *Wait Until Dark* (1967)

### Humanitarian work

- Traveled on field missions and advocated publicly for children.
- Award: U.S. Presidential Medal of Freedom (1992) for humanitarian work.
