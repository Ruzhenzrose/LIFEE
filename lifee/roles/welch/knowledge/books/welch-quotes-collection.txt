Jack Welch: Essential Quotes Collection
============================================================

ON LEADERSHIP

"Before you are a leader, success is all about growing yourself. When you become a leader, success is all about growing others."

"If you pick the right people and give them the opportunity to spread their wings, you almost don't have to manage them."

"The team with the best players wins."

"Giving people self-confidence is by far the most important thing I can do. Because then they will act."

"Good business leaders create a vision, articulate the vision, passionately own the vision and relentlessly drive it to completion."

"Control your own destiny or someone else will."

"Face reality as it is, not as it was or as you wish it to be."

"An organization's ability to learn, and translate that learning into action rapidly, is the ultimate competitive advantage."

"Leaders relentlessly upgrade their team, using every encounter as an opportunity to evaluate, coach, and build self-confidence."

"One of the jobs you have as a manager is to pump everyday self-confidence into your team to make them feel great."

ON CANDOR AND HONESTY

"Lack of candor blocks smart ideas, fast action, and good people from contributing everything they've got."

"You reinforce the behaviors that you reward. If you reward candor, you'll get it."

"If you are a leader and you are a manager, shame on you if people don't know where they stand. You have a moral obligation leading people's lives, talking about their future and not telling them where they stand."

"What would happen if for years and years you don't tell someone they are underperforming... And then a recession comes, and you need to fire the person, older and unprepared?"

"The biggest cowards are managers who don't let people know where they stand."

ON PEOPLE AND HIRING

"Executives spend more time on managing people and making people decisions than on anything else — and they should. No other decisions are so long lasting in their consequences or so difficult to unmake."

"Rigorous differentiation helps you become a real star and only real stars build great businesses."

"Differentiation provides dignity, develops future leaders, and creates winning companies."

"Take the top 20% of your employees and make them feel loved. Take the middle 70% and tell them what they need to do to get into the top 20%."

ON CAREER AND SUCCESS

"There's no such thing as work-life balance. There are work-life choices, and you make them, and they have consequences."

"Your work generally won't speak for itself. You must speak for yourself."

"Authenticity is such a value that people lose sight of. Learn from everybody, but be yourself."

"Always do more than you're asked for. Don't just do the homework assignment — do more."

"Everyone is a mentor. You see somebody speak, you say 'I like the way that person speaks,' you adapt your way of doing it."

"Life is too short to live someone else's dreams."

ON CHANGE AND INNOVATION

"Change before you have to."

"If the rate of change on the outside exceeds the rate of change on the inside, the end is near."

"Willingness to change is a strength, even if it means plunging part of the company into total confusion for a while."

"Don't manage — lead. Change before you have to. Don't play the game — change the rules of the game."

ON BUSINESS AND STRATEGY

"Business is about people. At the end of the day, people are what matter."

"Number one, cash is king... number two, communicate... number three, buy or bury the competition."

"What sets GE apart is a culture of learning and the boundless energy of its people."

"Make work fun, make it exciting, and reward the hell out of the ones who do the job."

"People want meaning in their work. They spend all their time at their work and they want to have purpose for it. And they want to know what's in it for them."

ON BUREAUCRACY

"I have a visceral hatred of bureaucracy. Bureaucracy hates change, could care less about the customer, loves complexity, is afraid of speed and incapable of it, and inspires no one."

"Bureaucracy is the enemy of speed. And speed is everything."

ON SELF-CONFIDENCE

"I got my self-confidence from my mother. She always told me I could be anything I wanted to be."

"My mother always said my stutter was because my brain worked faster than my mouth. That gave me confidence."

"Confidence is the secret. You have to believe in yourself to win."

"We need to do things that build people's self-confidence. It's all about praising others and getting excited about their victories. That's what makes a great leader."

ON TRUST

"You must gain the trust of your people. If you don't have their trust, you'll never be a great leader."

"Trust is built through consistent candor, following through on commitments, and demonstrating genuine care for people's development."

ON WINNING

"Winning lifts everyone it touches."

"The fact that people sort of sleepwalk through their jobs is just criminal."

"We've talked to over a million people in the last 10 years. And over and over again, we see frustrated workforces. We don't see an alignment between coming to work, winning, and having fun."
