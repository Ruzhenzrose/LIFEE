Jack Welch at Chick-fil-A Leadercast 2013: "Simply Lead"
May 10, 2013

Jack Welch spoke at the 2013 Chick-fil-A Leadercast conference themed "Simply Lead," delivering a presentation on leadership fundamentals to a large audience of business leaders. Below are his key points and quotes from the speech.

---

The 3 S's of Leadership:

Self-Confidence, Simplicity, Speed.

"Only with simplicity do you get speed. With speed you win the game."

Self-confidence is the starting point. A self-confident leader hires self-confident people, who in turn hire self-confident players. "If you hire a self-confident team, they hire self-confident players. The team that fields the best players wins."

---

On Focus and Commitment:

"Give it all you got. Have focus. Don't dabble."

The biggest advice Welch consistently gave: "Be in a job you love and over-deliver."

---

Teaching Moments:

"Use teaching moments to get everyone in the organization doing what you want."

Welch revealed that approximately 70% of his time as CEO was spent on teaching moments — using every interaction, every meeting, every decision as an opportunity to reinforce values and develop people.

---

On Transparency — The Cruelest Form of Management:

"If you are transparent, you have no right to be a manager if the employees don't know where they stand."

"For some reason people have trouble being transparent with their employees. It's the cruelest form of management."

Welch argued that not telling people where they stand — avoiding difficult conversations, giving vague feedback — is far crueler than honest, direct communication. Employees who don't know where they stand can't improve, can't make informed career decisions, and are blindsided when things go wrong.

---

Mission, Vision, and Behaviors:

Every leader must answer three fundamental questions:
1. Where are they going? (Mission)
2. Why are they going? (Vision)
3. How are they going to get there? (Behaviors)

---

Personnel Moves as Communication:

"You can give a thousand speeches, but every personnel move, one by one, will determine where you are."

"When you say something is important, you back it up with your very best people."

Actions speak louder than speeches. If you say innovation matters but don't put your best people on innovation, nobody believes you. Every promotion, every hire, every firing sends a signal about what the organization truly values.

---

The Performance-Values Matrix:

Welch reiterated his famous four-quadrant framework for evaluating employees:

1. High performance + High values = Put on the leadership track, reward generously
2. Low performance + Low values = Cut loose immediately
3. Low performance + High values = Give second and third chances to improve
4. High performance + Low values = The most dangerous category — cut them loose too, because they'll destroy your culture

"High performers without the values should also be cut loose. Otherwise, they'll ruin your culture."

---

On People Development:

Welch said that 70% of his role at GE was people development. Not strategy. Not finance. Not operations. People.

"The day you become a leader, your job is to take people who are already great and make them unbelievable."

---

On Celebrating Wins:

"Have small celebrations for every little victory on the way to reaching your goal."

"Excite people. Send them to training."

Welch believed in making work fun and celebratory. Recognition didn't have to be grand — small, frequent acknowledgments of progress were more powerful than occasional large rewards.

---

Core Leadership Philosophy:

The speech reinforced themes Welch had championed throughout his career:
- Leaders are made, not born
- Candor is the most important organizational value
- People decisions are the most important decisions a leader makes
- Simplicity enables speed, which enables winning
- Every interaction is a teaching moment
- Self-confidence is contagious — and so is its absence
